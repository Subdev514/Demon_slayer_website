Demon slayer

The story takes place in Taishō-era Japan. It follows Tanjiro Kamado and his sister Nezuko Kamado as they seek a cure for Nezuko's demon curse. Tanjiro and Nezuko become entangled in the affairs of a secret society, known as the Demon Slayer Corps, that has been waging a secret war against demons for centuries. The demons are former humans who sold their humanity in exchange for power. They feed on humans and possess supernatural abilities such as super strength, magic, and regeneration. Demons can only be killed if they're decapitated with weapons crafted from an alloy known as Sun Steel, injected with poison extracted from wisteria flowers, or exposed to sunlight. The Demon Slayers, on the other hand, are entirely human; however, they employ special breathing techniques, known as "Breathing Styles", which grant them superhuman strength and increased resistance.

Why demon slayer?

Demon Slayer has been praised for its gorgeous animation, particularly its dynamic action sequences where every frame feels like a piece of art. The stunning visuals are a credit to ufotable, the animation studio behind the anime adaptation. But the series’ appeal is not the art alone. While “Demon Slayer” features familiar manga tropes, its characters and story have also been a major part of its success.The anime’s popularity led to an increased interest in the manga series, which was still ongoing when the first season had finished airing in Japan. Those curious about how the story continues beyond the anime series turned to the source material. Books from the “Demon Slayer” manga series topped Japanese sales charts in 2020 and was attributed as one of the factors that helped boost the entire comic market.

Movie: mugan train

The movie is more than just an anime film about members of the Demon Slayer Corps trying to protect the passengers of a train from being eaten by a demon. It’s the movie that saved the Japanese box office during the pandemic, Earning more than $350 million at Japan’s box office during its opening weekend, surpassing not only the recordas the highest grossing Japanese film of all time, but the fourth-highest grossing film of 2020. More than simply a continuation of the series’ broad plot, the film is based directly on an arc of Koyoharu Gotouge’s original manga, and is intended as a canonical bridge between the events of season 1 and 2.

Ufotable

While mostly known for adapting games and light novels, anime studio Ufotable became a worldwide phenomenon with its work on Demon Slayer. Ufotable as a studio has always pushed the boundary when it comes to integrating CG in traditional animation and this time was no different. With the massive success of Demon Slayer, The company has become well-known in the anime community. founded in Nakano, Tokyo in October of 2000 by former employees of Telecom Animation Film, led by Hikaru Kondo, the company started with 90 staff members, but as of 2016, that number has increased to 200. who knows how much that number has beocome today.Although in March 2019, it was reported that a search was performed on Ufotable's offices due to alleged tax evasion. The next month, it was reported that Ufotable owed ¥400 million in taxes.

What's next?

The arc follows on from the Mugen Train animated film and is set in the Yoshiwara red-light district. It will again be directed by Haruo Sotozaki, with character designs from Akira Matsushima, and produced at Ufotable. It is said to release in october of 2021.
